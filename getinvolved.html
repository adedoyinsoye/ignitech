According to the UNICEF, Nigeria has 10.5 million out-of-school children-the world’s highest number.

Children from underserved and low-income communities such as Makoko, Ajegunle and the likes are unfortunately part of these 10.5 million out-of-school children

A 13-year-old canoe paddler, Dudu Ebince told The Nation that he could not remember if he ever attended school.

“I may have gone to school when i was little, but I can’t remember. I am a canoe paddler, I ferry people from this bank to anywhere they want to go inside Makoko and make between N1000 and N1500 per-day depending on the day”
